The Film
Five Acts. Five Minutes. One Nation.

CAMPAIGN FILM — "THE SEED OF SOVEREIGNTY" · DURATION: 5:00 · ASPECT RATIO: 16:9 MASTER / 9:16 SOCIAL CUT · FORMAT: 4K RAW · GRADE: DAVINCI RESOLVE · COLOR BIBLE: GHANA SOIL SYSTEM

The Core Thesis

Ghana's Big Push initiative is not a policy announcement. It is the restoration of a severed connection — between the farmer's hand and the mother's kitchen, between the soil and the shelf. This film makes that connection visible, sacred, and cinematic.

The Emotional Arc

From ancestral soil to industrial sovereignty. The film opens in whisper (the farmer alone) and closes in thunder (the united front). Every act escalates — from one seed to one nation. The audience should feel the weight of generations building.

The VFX Conceit

Historical Ghanaian leaders appear as golden-amber shimmering presences — not ghosts, not holograms, but ancestral light. Each leader's appearance is brief, dignified, and compositionally precise. The VFX reads as memory made visible.

"They told us a nation is built on gold and oil. But the Osagyefo knew — a nation is built on what it can feed itself."

Act I of V
The Ancestors in the Soil

Scene 01 of 12
THE FIRST STRIKE — Kweku's Field, Upper East Region
Timecode: 00:00 — 00:20 · 20 seconds
Emotional Tone
Sacred. Ancient. Geological. The earth before the story begins.
Dominant Colour Bias
Ochre (#B8860B) / Dusty Gold (#D4A017) / Deep Crimson shadows
Contrast Strategy
Maximum earth tones. The red soil against golden light. High saturation on the dust cloud. The world is warm and primal.
Lighting Temperature
2800K — Pre-dawn golden hour. Long shadows. No fill light. The sun alone.
Key Colour Anchors
Laterite earth Dawn light Wooden hoe Kweku's hands
Lens Direction
100mm f/2.8 macro for soil texture. 85mm f/1.4 for hands. 24mm for wide field reveal.

EXTREME CLOSE-UP. Cracked, rich red laterite earth fills the frame entirely. The texture is geological — ancient, layered, alive. Hold for three seconds. The audience sees only earth.

A wooden hoe enters frame from screen right. The blade is worn smooth from decades of use. It strikes the ground. The impact sends a fine cloud of ochre dust rising in slow motion (240fps). The sound reverberates — not metallic, but deep, like a heartbeat.

Camera Direction
Macro lens (100mm f/2.8) for the soil texture. Dolly back slowly on the strike to reveal the hoe. Rack focus from soil surface to the hoe blade mid-swing. Shoot at 240fps for the dust cloud, conform to 24fps in post. The slow-motion dust is sacred — it is the breath of the earth.

CUT TO: CLOSE-UP on the hands of elderly farmer KWEKU. His hands are dark, deeply lined, soil pressed into every crease. He lifts a single tomato seed between his thumb and forefinger. Holds it against the dawn light. The seed is almost translucent — the light passes through it like stained glass.

Audio
Lone seperewa — a single melody line, unaccompanied. The notes are deliberate, unhurried, ancient. Wind across the field beneath it. A bird call — speckled mousebird, native to the Northern Region. Natural ambient only. No synthesised sound.
Scene 02 of 12
THE OSAGYEFO APPEARS — Ancestral Presence
Timecode: 00:20 — 00:45 · 25 seconds
Emotional Tone
Reverent. Luminous. The founding vision made visible beside the living farmer.
Dominant Colour Bias
Dusty Gold (#D4A017) — the golden VFX register dominates
Lighting Temperature
2800K ambient + VFX amber overlay at comp level
Key Colour Anchors
VFX amber glow Earth floor Tomato leaf

Kweku lowers the seed toward the earth. As his hand descends, the air beside him shimmers — not like heat haze, but like sunlight passing through water. A figure forms.

VFX Direction
Dr. Kwame Nkrumah materialises at 40% opacity, rendered in golden-amber light particles. Not ghostly — luminous. The effect reads as ancestral presence, not supernatural haunting. Nkrumah's hand extends and settles over Kweku's as the seed enters the soil. Golden particles flow from his hand into the earth. Total VFX: 8 seconds. Reference: shimmer of light on moving water at dawn. Comp in DaVinci Fusion or After Effects. Clean plate required.

MEDIUM WIDE. Kweku kneels in the soil. The shimmering form of Nkrumah stands beside him — one hand on his shoulder, the other gesturing across the field toward the horizon. The composition reads as a painting. The frame holds. The figures do not move. The wind moves the tomato leaves around them.

Voiceover — Deep, Resonant Male Voice (Akan cadence)
"They told us a nation is built on gold and oil. But the Osagyefo knew — a nation is not built on what lies beneath the earth. It is built on what rises from it. On what a people can grow with their own hands. On what a nation can feed itself."
Audio
The seperewa melody deepens. A single fontomfrom drum enters — one beat, resonating, on the word "rises." The drum and the seperewa breathe together. Wind intensifies slightly on the horizon shot.

Camera Direction

TIME-LAPSE. Kweku's single field multiplies. The drone pulls back as adjacent fields appear — more farmers, more rows, more red against ochre. The landscape transforms from one man's plot to a regional industry.

CUT TO: MEDIUM SHOT. A shimmering figure at the edge of the expanded farmland. Fl. Lt. Jerry John Rawlings — rendered in golden-amber VFX — points toward the horizon. His posture is commanding, certain. He does not look at camera. He looks at the future.

VFX Direction
Rawlings rendered at 35% opacity with warm amber particle system. Particles drift in the direction he points. His presence lasts 6 seconds — appearing on a cut, dissolving into the dust of the expanding field. The transition from VFX particles to actual airborne dust is seamless.
Audio
Music shifts decisively — driving percussive Afrobeats enter. Talking drum (atumpan) sets the rhythm. Hi-hat pattern from a modern kit layers over traditional percussion. The beat is propulsive, forward-moving.
Voiceover
"One field became ten. Ten became a hundred. The leaders who walked this soil understood — a revolution is not a moment. It is a harvest that takes generations to grow."

Audio
Percussive beat continues but adds factory rhythm — clang-press of the canning machine, hiss of steam sealing, rattle of tins on belt. These industrial sounds are mixed into the beat, not separate. The factory is an instrument.
Voiceover

THE RELAY OF LIGHT — Leader Montage

Camera Direction
Start from high drone (100m), truck as small red artery against the landscape. Descend to truck level over 10 seconds. Cut to interior: driver's hands on wheel, road through windshield. Anamorphic for the highway — the format makes the road cinematic.

INTERIOR, CAB. The driver glances at his side mirror. In the reflection — subtle, fleeting — the shimmering forms of Kofi Busia and Hilla Limann stand at the roadside, watching the truck pass, fading as it moves.

VFX Direction
Busia and Limann at 25% opacity — barely visible, almost imagined. They appear in the mirror reflection only, not in the direct landscape. An Easter egg for the attentive. Duration: 3 seconds, dissolving as the truck moves forward.
Audio
Engine rumble — deep diesel, felt in the chest. Percussive Afrobeats pulse continues with new rhythmic element: tyre pattern creates a steady thrum. Wind rushing across cab. The sounds of motion.
Voiceover

Dominant Colour Bias

ESTABLISHING. Jubilee House exterior at dusk. Building lit warm gold against darkening sky. Ghana flag moves slowly in evening breeze. Hold three seconds before we enter.

INTERIOR. Presidential office. Rich wood. Warm light. On the desk: the Big Push Initiative report, sanguine-red tomato emblem on cover. President John Dramani Mahama reviews the document. He turns a page. His reading glasses catch the light.

Camera Direction
Dolly in slowly from wide to medium. No handheld. No urgency. Authority from stillness. 50mm f/2, report sharp, background warm bokeh. For the declaration: locked-off tripod, 85mm f/2. President fills frame chest to crown. Tomato and tin visible in lower third.

The President stands. Moves to the front of the desk. In his left hand, a fresh tomato — red, ripe, from the Northern Region. In his right, the canned product — the Ghanaian-branded tin. He holds them both at chest height. The raw material and the finished product. The past and the present.

He looks directly into camera. The only moment in the film where a subject addresses the audience. The intimacy is deliberate. He is not performing. He is speaking.

Presidential Voice — Direct to Camera
"For too long, we grew the tomato and bought back the tin. For too long, the harvest left this nation on a lorry and returned on a ship — wrapped in a foreign language. We have laid the tracks. We have built the factories. We have connected the farmer to the shelf. This is the Big Push. This is not a slogan. This is a promise — from this soil, through these hands, to every kitchen in Ghana. This is our Ghana. And this — is our tomato."

He places the tomato and tin together on the desk. They sit side by side. The camera holds. The farmer's fruit and the nation's product, united in the same frame.

Audio
Silence throughout the speech. Only the President's voice and room tone. After "tomato" — a single fontomfrom beat. Deep. Felt in the ribcage. Then the seperewa melody from Act I returns, joined by strings — a full orchestral arrangement building.

WIDE SHOT, GOLDEN HOUR. An open field at dawn — the same ochre earth from Act I. Standing in a single line, facing camera: Farmer Kweku. A factory worker in bone-white uniform. A truck driver. Mother Efua with her youngest child on her hip. At the centre — President Mahama.

They stand still. They do not smile. They do not perform. They stand the way people stand when they know what they have built. The golden-hour light falls equally across all. No one is lit more than another. The composition is democratic.

VFX Direction — Most Complex Shot
Behind the living line, historical leaders shimmer into view — Nkrumah, Rawlings, Kufuor, Atta Mills, Busia, Limann — rendered in full golden light at 50% opacity. They stand like an ancestral chorus. Golden particles move gently, like fireflies at dusk. Effect builds over 5 seconds. Clean plates required for all positions. Estimated comp time: 40 hours.
Camera Direction
200mm telephoto from distance — compresses the line, figures appear closer, more unified. Shallow depth turns background to pure golden bokeh. Slow zoom out to 24mm wide over 5 seconds, revealing full field, full line, full ancestral chorus. Optical zoom only.

The line holds for two beats. Then — CUT TO BLACK. On Night Indigo field, text appears in sequence. Each line arrives on a hard cut — no fades:

GHANA TOMATOES

THE SEED OF SOVEREIGNTY

Grown by our History. Powered by our People.

Audio
Full orchestral swell — strings, brass, seperewa harmonised by full ensemble. Fontomfrom heartbeat. Atumpan pulse. This is the only moment where the full musical palette deploys. The swell reaches peak on title card. Then silence. Then a single chime — the Ghana Beyond Aid motif — rings once, clean, resonant. Hold silence for two seconds. Cut to black. End.
